Skills

I build at the intersection of machine learning, statistical rigor, and human cognition. My work spans production AI systems, applied ML modeling, and research-grade experiments comparing how humans and language models make decisions under uncertainty.

Core Competencies

I use ML in two modes — production and research — and I think they make each other better. In production contexts, I care about models that are interpretable and defensible, not just accurate. In research contexts, I care about models that reveal something true about the data, not just fit it. The methods are often the same; what changes is the question I'm asking them to answer.

A product in production proves you understand the full problem — the data layer, the API contracts, the user experience, the failure modes. Dialed was my proof of concept for this. My thesis asks a question I think will only get more important: when LLMs deliberate in groups, do they actually make better decisions — or do they just agree with each other? That question lives at the intersection of cognitive science, AI evaluation, and multi-agent systems. I want to take it into alignment research, into industry roles building human-AI interfaces, and deeper into graduate work — not as three separate paths, but as the same problem examined from different angles.
