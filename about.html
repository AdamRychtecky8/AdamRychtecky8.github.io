About Me

I came to data science through the brain. At UCSB I studied both Statistics and Psychological & Brain Sciences — not because I had a plan, but because both kept pulling at the same question: how do systems, human or artificial, actually make sense of the world? Research gave me my first real answer. I watched a model optimize flawlessly while understanding nothing. That gap stuck with me, and it still shapes how I think about building things.

I’m finishing my degrees at UCSB and starting the MS in Data Science at UW this fall. I’m early in my career and I know it — but the problems I keep gravitating toward are the ones where being technically sharp isn’t enough on its own.

Where I’m Headed

Systems of Intelligence

The problems worth solving are too complex for any single approach. I’m drawn to designing systems where AI, ML, and human judgment each play to their strengths — and together get somewhere none of them could alone.

AI as Collaborator

I don’t think the goal is AI that does the job. It’s AI that makes the human doing the job better than either could be independently. That distinction shapes how I think about every system I build.

Hard Problems, Done Properly

I’m drawn to problems that are genuinely difficult — and I care about solving them correctly, not just quickly. There’s a specific satisfaction in working through something hard until it finally gives way. I chase that feeling.

Outside of work, I try to read as much as I can — sci-fi mostly for the fun of it, and because imagining what’s possible is its own kind of thinking; philosophy because life is confusing and it helps; and ML books for everything the classroom didn’t get to.
