About Me

I’m a senior at UC Santa Barbara pursuing a B.S. in Statistics and Data Science with a double major in Psychological and Brain Sciences. My work sits at the intersection of statistical modeling, machine learning, and the science of human decision-making.

As an Executive Research Assistant in the Vision and Image Understanding Lab, I’ve led large-scale psychophysics experiments comparing human and AI visual perception, helping build datasets and analysis pipelines used in published research. My honors thesis extends this into AI collective intelligence — examining whether ensembles of LLMs approximate human group decision-making heuristics.
