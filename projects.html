Court-Vision

Tested whether the NBA’s three-point shift was mathematically justified across 4.2 million shot records and 21 seasons, all processed with PySpark on Google Cloud Dataproc. Combined zone efficiency analysis, a LASSO-regularized shot-make classifier, season-level time series, and PCA with K-Means clustering of player profiles. The sharpest result: the mid-range carries a built-in efficiency penalty, and the mid-range specialist archetype collapsed from 37% of player-seasons to under 8% as the balanced modern scorer took over.

When Human and AI Crowds Agree Too Much

Honors thesis research · UCSB Vision & Image Understanding Lab

Compared how groups of humans and groups of LLMs make collective decisions on an identical target-detection task, benchmarked against the Bayesian ideal observer. Measured individual sensitivity with Signal Detection Theory, then built majority-vote ensembles and computed error correlation across every agent pair. The sharpest result: adding agents stops helping after about five because their errors are correlated, so twelve agents are worth far less than twelve independent votes.

Python Signal Detection Theory Bootstrap scikit-learn LLM APIs

Modeling Perceptual Decisions: Humans vs. LLMs

PSTAT 231 Final Project — class project using real honors thesis data

Predicted whether humans and LLMs would respond “present” or “absent” on identical visual detection trials — modeling agent behavior, not ground truth. Fit a GAM as an interpretable baseline and CatBoost to capture feature interactions the additive model cannot. SHAP analysis revealed the sharpest human–LLM difference: LLMs treat trial clarity as a reliable cue; humans show almost no sensitivity to the same signal.

Wavelength

Building a quote-retrieval app that matches a written prompt to the quote fitting a user’s emotional intent, not just its keywords. An LLM tags each quote across 18 features and generates the candidate slates that train a LightGBM reranker, which then serves results at query time with zero API cost. The design point: the LLM is a teacher that trains its own cheaper replacement.

PM2.5 and Alzheimer’s Disease: Mechanisms, Risk, and Economic Burden

Undergraduate systematic literature review · WRIT W109ST, UCSB

Synthesized 12 peer-reviewed studies (2015–2024) across three research questions: biological mechanisms, public health implications, and economic burden. Key findings: PM2.5 exposure linked to neuronal loss, phosphorylated-tau elevation, and amyloid-β deposits in post-mortem tissue; road proximity raised AD hazard ratios to 2.05; Sweden and Sichuan attributed 5–16.5% of AD cases to PM2.5 at ~€213,000 per case.
