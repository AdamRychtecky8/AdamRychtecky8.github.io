Ensemble Decision-Making Under Correlation: Humans vs. LLM Groups

Honors thesis research · UCSB Vision & Image Understanding Lab

Replicated and extended Juni & Eckstein (2015) across 507,600 trials, comparing human groups and LLM ensembles (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini) on a 2AFC visual detection task. Groups of 12 outperform individuals by up to 15% — but gains plateau at n≈7, explained by high error correlation in 35–40% of model pairs.

Python Signal Detection Theory Bootstrap scikit-learn LLM APIs

Modeling Perceptual Decisions: Humans vs. LLMs

PSTAT 231 Final Project — class project using real honors thesis data

Predicted whether humans and LLMs would respond “present” or “absent” on identical visual detection trials — modeling agent behavior, not ground truth. Fit a GAM as an interpretable baseline and CatBoost to capture feature interactions the additive model cannot. SHAP analysis revealed the sharpest human–LLM difference: LLMs treat trial clarity as a reliable cue; humans show almost no sensitivity to the same signal.

PM2.5 and Alzheimer’s Disease: Mechanisms, Risk, and Economic Burden

Undergraduate systematic literature review · WRIT W109ST, UCSB

Synthesized 12 peer-reviewed studies (2015–2024) across three research questions: biological mechanisms, public health implications, and economic burden. Key findings: PM2.5 exposure linked to neuronal loss, phosphorylated-tau elevation, and amyloid-β deposits in post-mortem tissue; road proximity raised AD hazard ratios to 2.05; Sweden and Sichuan attributed 5–16.5% of AD cases to PM2.5 at ~€213,000 per case.
